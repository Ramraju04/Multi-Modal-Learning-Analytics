Under the Hood

The MMLA Architecture combines state-of-the-art Computer Vision with Deep Reinforcement Learning.

1. Multimodal Sensor Fusion

[Video Stream] + [Log Data] -> Fusion Layer

We capture raw data streams from simulated sensors and process them in real-time.

  • Visual Data: Gaze tracking & facial landmarks (CNN-based).
  • Behavioral Logs: Click patterns & dwell time (LSTM-based).
  • Assessment: Real-time quiz performance normalization.

2. The DRL Agent (DQN)

State(S) -> Policy(π) -> Action(A)

A Deep Q-Network acts as the pedagogical brain, optimizing for long-term retention.

  • Algorithm: Double DQN with Prioritized Experience Replay.
  • Reward Function: Weighted sum of Engagement + Learning Gain.
  • Latency: < 50ms inference time on standard GPU.

3. Adaptive Curriculum

[Content Pool] -> [Personalized Sequence]

Content is dynamically sequenced based on the student's unique "Knowledge State".

  • Dynamic Difficulty: Adjusts challenge level on the fly.
  • Format Switching: Video -> Quiz -> Reading based on fatigue.
  • Scalable: Supports thousands of concurrent learner sessions.
